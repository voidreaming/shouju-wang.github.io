Research Statement

Shouju Wang · University of Hawai'i at Mānoa

Overview

My research centers on building trustworthy and privacy-preserving AI systems, with a focus on large language model (LLM) agents that operate in socially complex, real-world environments. I am particularly interested in understanding and enforcing contextual integrity—the idea that information flows should respect the norms of the contexts in which they occur—within multimodal LLM and VLM agents.

Research Themes

LLM Privacy & Contextual Integrity

As LLM-powered agents become embedded in everyday workflows—scheduling meetings, drafting emails, managing personal data—they inevitably encounter sensitive information. My work investigates how these agents can be designed and evaluated to respect privacy norms grounded in contextual integrity theory. I have developed benchmarks and mitigation strategies for realistic privacy evaluation of LLM agents, resulting in work published at EMNLP 2025 Findings ("Privacy in Action"). My ongoing project, MPCI-Bench, extends this line of inquiry to multimodal settings, providing the first pairwise contextual integrity benchmark for LLM/VLM agents.

Agent Alignment & Safety

Beyond privacy, I am broadly interested in the alignment of autonomous agents—ensuring that their behavior reflects human values and intentions across diverse interaction contexts. This includes studying how agents handle conflicting instructions, navigate ambiguous social norms, and balance helpfulness with safety constraints. I believe that grounding alignment in well-established social theories like contextual integrity offers a principled path forward.

Fair Graph Learning

My earlier work addressed fairness in graph-based machine learning, specifically in anomaly detection. I developed DECAF-GAD, a causal inference framework that mitigates bias in graph neural network-based anomaly detectors while preserving detection accuracy. This work was presented as an oral paper at ECAI 2025, and it reflects my broader commitment to building AI systems that are both effective and equitable.

Future Directions

Looking ahead, I aim to develop formal frameworks and practical tools that enable LLM agents to reason about and respect information norms in complex, multimodal settings. Key questions driving my research include:

How can we formalize and operationalize contextual integrity for autonomous agents that perceive, reason about, and act upon multimodal information?

I envision a future where AI agents can dynamically adapt their behavior based on contextual norms—understanding not just what information they have access to, but whether and how it is appropriate to use it. This requires advances in norm-aware reasoning, privacy-preserving architectures, and rigorous evaluation methodologies.

I am actively seeking collaborators who share an interest in trustworthy AI, privacy, and agent alignment. Please feel free to reach out!
