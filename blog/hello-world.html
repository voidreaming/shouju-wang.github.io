Hello World: Starting My PhD Journey

March 2026 · 3 min read

It feels surreal to finally write this. After four years at Wuhan University and an incredible internship at Microsoft Research Asia, I've officially begun my PhD at the University of Hawai'i at Mānoa.

Why Privacy & LLM Agents?

During my time at MSRA, I worked on understanding how LLM-powered agents handle privacy in realistic settings. The more I dug into the problem, the more I realized how fundamental the gap is between what these models know about privacy norms and how they actually behave when deployed as agents. This question — the privacy judgment-action gap — became the seed of my PhD research.

What I'm Working On

My current research focuses on multimodal contextual integrity for LLM and VLM agents. Contextual integrity, originally proposed by Helen Nissenbaum, provides a framework for reasoning about privacy as appropriate information flow. I'm extending this framework to multimodal settings where agents process images, text, and structured data simultaneously.

Privacy is not about secrecy. It's about the appropriate flow of information in context.

Life in Hawai'i

Moving from Wuhan to Honolulu has been quite the change. The weather is perpetually beautiful, the campus is surrounded by mountains, and the research environment here is warm and collaborative. I'm grateful for the opportunity to work with Prof. Haopeng Zhang and the wonderful people in our lab.

This blog will be a space for me to share research thoughts, paper readings, and reflections on the PhD journey. Stay tuned!
